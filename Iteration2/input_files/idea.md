**Title: Residual-Based Identification of Anomalous Electronic Coupling in MX2 TMDs**

This research moves beyond simple DOS-thresholding by quantifying the "Electronic Overperformance" of transition metals within the MX2 structural landscape. We will execute a multivariate linear regression where `dos_at_fermi` is the dependent variable, modeled as a function of `volume`, `spacegroup` (via one-hot encoding), and `log1p(energy_above_hull)`. By calculating the positive residuals of this model, we isolate materials where the transition metal's electronic contribution significantly exceeds the expectations set by their structural geometry and thermodynamic stability. This approach identifies "electronic outliers"—materials that achieve high DOS not merely due to favorable symmetry or volume, but through intrinsic compositional properties that may foster enhanced electron-phonon coupling. By ranking candidates by these regression residuals, we provide a prioritized list of metallic candidates (`band_gap` = 0.0 eV) that possess the highest potential for superconductivity while remaining agnostic to the bias of structural prevalence. This methodology effectively decouples compositional excellence from structural geometry, offering a refined, physically grounded path for experimental target identification.
