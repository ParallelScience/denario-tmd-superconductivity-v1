By including highly metastable materials in the final prioritized list without a constraint or filter for synthesizability, the analysis identifies theoretical electronic outliers that may be physically impossible to realize experimentally. Consequently, the conclusions regarding these materials as "targets for experimental validation" are affected, as the dataset lacks the necessary information (e.g., phonon dispersion calculations or synthesis feasibility metrics) to distinguish between metastable phases that are experimentally accessible and those that are dynamically unstable.
